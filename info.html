About eWaste Management

eWaste, or electronic waste, refers to discarded electronic devices and components. As technology evolves rapidly, the accumulation of eWaste has become a significant environmental issue. Proper eWaste management involves collecting, recycling, and safely disposing of electronic products to reduce environmental impact.

Why eWaste Management is Important

eWaste contains hazardous materials such as lead, mercury, and cadmium that can contaminate soil and water if not disposed of correctly. Recycling eWaste helps recover valuable materials like gold, silver, and copper, reducing the need for mining and conserving natural resources. Proper management also prevents the release of harmful substances, protecting human health and the environment.

How You Can Help

You can contribute to eWaste management by:

  • Donating or selling old electronics instead of discarding them.
  • Using certified eWaste recycling facilities.
  • Spreading awareness about the importance of eWaste management.
